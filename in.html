Welcome to Paws of Hope,

A sanctuary where every animal finds a second chance at life. Our shelter is dedicated to rescuing, rehabilitating, and rehoming abandoned and neglected animals. At Paws of Hopee, we believe that every paw matters, and our mission is to provide a safe haven for animals in need. Our team of passionate volunteers and staff work tirelessly to ensure that each animal receives the love, care, and medical attention they deserve. From playful puppies and kittens to senior pets looking for a cozy retirement home, we strive to match each animal with a loving family. Our shelter offers a variety of programs, including adoption events, spay and neuter clinics, and educational workshops to promote responsible pet ownership. We also collaborate with local communities to raise awareness about animal welfare and the importance of compassion towards all living beings. At Paws of Hopee, we envision a world where every animal is treated with kindness and respect. Join us in our mission to give these animals a brighter future. Whether you're looking to adopt, volunteer, or donate, your support makes a difference. Together, we can create a community where every animal is cherished and loved.

Information on Animal Shelters
Animal shelters are facilities that provide temporary housing and care for stray, lost, abandoned, or surrendered animals, primarily dogs and cats. Here are some key points about animal shelters:
  • Purpose: Animal shelters aim to find permanent homes for animals, provide medical care, and reduce the number of stray animals on the streets.
    •
  • Types of Animals: Most shelters house dogs and cats, but some also take in other animals like rabbits, birds, and small mammals.
    •
  • Intake and Surrender: Animals enter shelters for various reasons, including being found as strays, rescued from cruelty situations, or surrendered by owners who can no longer care for them.
    •
  • Adoption: Shelters work to match animals with suitable adoptive families. Approximately 4.1 million shelter animals are adopted each year in the U.S. alone.
    •
  • Euthanasia: Unfortunately, due to overpopulation and limited resources, some shelters may have to euthanize animals. However, the number of animals euthanized has been decreasing over the years.
    •
  • Volunteering and Donations: Shelters often rely on volunteers and donations to operate. Many shelters welcome help with animal care, administrative tasks, and fundraising efforts
    •

The Founding of Paws of Hopee

  • In 2015, a group of dedicated animal lovers in the town of Castletroy, Ireland, came together with a shared vision: to create a safe haven for abandoned and neglected animals. This vision became a reality with the establishment of Paws of Hopee.

  • The Visionaries

  • The shelter was founded by a small team of individuals who were united by their love for animals and their desire to make a positive impact in their community. Each member brought unique skills and perspectives, from veterinary care to business acumen and advocacy.

  • The Early Days

  • Starting the shelter was a challenging endeavor. The founders faced numerous obstacles, including securing funding and finding a suitable location. They organized community fundraisers, applied for grants, and even contributed their own resources to bring their vision to life. Their efforts led them to an old, unused building on the outskirts of Castletroy, which they transformed into a welcoming space for animals in need.

  • Opening Day

  • In the summer of 2015, Paws of Hopee officially opened its doors. The local community rallied around the shelter, donating supplies, volunteering their time, and spreading the word. The shelter's first residents included a variety of animals, all of whom quickly found loving homes thanks to the shelter's dedicated adoption efforts.

  • Growth and Impact

  • Over the years, Paws of Hopee has grown significantly. The shelter now features a modern facility with separate areas for different types of animals, a veterinary clinic, and a spacious outdoor play area. They have also expanded their services to include educational programs, spay/neuter clinics, and partnerships with local schools and organizations.

  • A Bright Future

  • Today, Paws of Hopee continues to thrive, thanks to the unwavering support of the Castletroy community and the tireless efforts of its founders and volunteers. The shelter has become a beacon of hope for countless animals, providing them with the care and love they deserve.
